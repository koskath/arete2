# Value-Adding Activities

Warehousing should aim to provide value‑adding services as well as to minimise operating costs. The four principal value‑adding activities are, first, creating and breaking bulk consignments in order to achieve better transport economies; second, combining freight, which can be seen as a form of postponement; and third, smoothing supply to meet demand by using buffer stocks, for example in leagile supply chains. Source: Mangan et al. (2021). 12
